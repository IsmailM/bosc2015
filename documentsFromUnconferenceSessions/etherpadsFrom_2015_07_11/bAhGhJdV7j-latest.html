Effective access to and communication with users
Brad, Carole, Aurelie, Sara, Herve

What are examples of software that failed?

Sara volunteers an example from her own experience. GenomeSpace needs additional recipes from users -- what are they doing and can they contribute back to it? Do not have a forum or mailing list so people can contribute back.

Suggestion from Carole -- go out and actually work with users in real time. Users are in the lab. Focus on graduate students and other folks with time/interest.

Aurelie -- need to build a relationship with users so it's easier to impose on their time to get feedback. Need to find a very easy way to get information from them since they're not used to communicating in this way. Different people have different ways of communicating and might need multiple ways: forum, mailing lists, wiki, in person.

Sara -- need to find people that are have lots of interest. Mailing list is not open as a place for discussion. More setup for bug reports and discussion amongst developers. You need a specific type of culture that makes it easy to discuss these things, or pointers to go the right place for asking.
 
 Specific webinars that are short and focused to get people involved -- can't have a bigger talk with too many things at the same time.
 
 Problem is when people fail and are nervous and might not submit a bug report. Fear of looking like they don't know what they're doing. Is there a way to create a culture that it's easier for people to do this?
 
 Herve -- make it easier/lightweight to submit report and fixes.
 Carole -- better FAQs
 Sara -- biologists aren't using GitHub
 Herve -- move to QA sites like BioStars

Need to be friendly and listen to users, and be responsive-- can get tough when you get busy.
